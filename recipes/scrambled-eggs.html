Scrambled Eggs

This is not just any scrambled egg recipe, it's one from world-renowned chef Gordon Ramsay. This is arguably one of the best tasting scrambled eggs you will ever taste in your life, and some say that those who are fortunate enough to be able to make this dish temporarily went to heaven when they tasted it. Don't let the amount of ingredients fool you, as this scrambled egg recipe is one of the hardest ones to master, being equal in difficulty to the world famous Japanese omelette on demi-rice. However, if you are able to follow these directions to a T you will be thanking yourself once it's all completed. Are you ready in mind and body to make this dish? Lets find out soon.

Ingredients

  • 6 Cold, organic free range eggs
  • 1.5 tbs Butter- divided
  • Kosher Salt and fresh ground pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp Crème fraîche
  • Chives to garnish

Directions

  1. Crack 6 cold eggs into a non stick, small saucepan.
  2. Add the butter.
  3. Turn the heat on to med-high.
  4. Stir continuously with a rubber spatula—don't whisk—making sure to scrape the bottom of the pan.
  5. After 30 seconds, take the pan off the heat. Keep stirring. After about 10 seconds, put back on the heat. Repeat for about 3 minutes. (Adjust heat if they are cooking too fast.)
  6. Off the heat, season the eggs lightly with salt & pepper. Stir in 1 tsp of crème fraîche
  7. Plate and garnish with chopped chives.
